How to Build Footings

The first thing to do after calculating your job materials is set up your construction area ready for your project. Please try to remember to work safely ensuring injuries are avoided.

Definitions

Articulated Masonry -This means masonry construction in which special provisions have been made for movement by articulation.

Clad Frame - This means timber or metal frame construction with exterior timber or sheet wall cladding that is not sensitive to minor movement and includes substructure masonry walls up to 1.5metres.

Controlled Fill - This mean material that has been placed and compacted in layers with compacting equipment (such as a vibrating plate) within a defined moisture range to a defined density requirement.

Finished Ground Level - This means the ground level adjacent to footing systems at the completion of construction and landscaping.

Footing - This means construction that transfers the load from the building to the foundation.

Concrete Footings - Construction
  1. The base of the footing must generally be level.
  2. The base must be free of loose earth, tree roots, mud or debris immediately before pouring concrete.
  3. Topsoil containing grass roots must be removed from the area on which footings will rest.
  4. The depth of footings must not be less than 300mm for loose sand sites or sites subject to wind erosion.
  5. Before excavation, clearly mark out the location of footings according to certified engineer drawings.
  6. Before pouring concrete ensure all footings have been certified by authority.
