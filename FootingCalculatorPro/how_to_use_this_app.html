How to Use Footings Calculator


Settings
If you are using the Calculator for business purposes, it is a good idea to set the preferences to Estimate & Quote. The user preferences also allows you to add & edited the default cost factors for you individual use. The costing should be set if you are using it to quote on projects.

Piers - Calculator
  1. Enter into the first box the diameter of your piers.
  2. Enter into the second box the depth of the first pier.
  3. Press the button "3. Press to Add Calculator Answers" to add your depth of the first pier hole. Then just keep adding the depths of each pier and press the button to keep adding the answers.

NOTE: The calculator will keep adding the inputs to the list amount that is required for all your quantities. The total answer can be saved to the job folder for each construction project. If you need to delete or edit an answer, just click on that line.

Pads - Calculator
  1. Enter into the first box the Length of your pad footing.
  2. Enter into the second box the Width of the pad footing.
  3. Enter into the third box the Depth of the pad footing.
  4. Press the button "4. Press to Add Calculator Answers" to add your first answer. Then just keep adding the depths of each pier and press the button to keep adding the answers.


NOTE: The calculator will keep adding the inputs to the list amount that is required for all your quantities. The total answer can be saved to the job folder for each construction project. If you need to delete or edit an answer, just click on that line.

Strip Footings - Calculator
  1. Enter into the first box the Width of your strip footing.
  2. Enter into the second box the Depth of the strip footing.
  3. Enter into the third box the Length of the strip footing.
  4. Press the button "4. Press to Add Calculator Answers" to add your first answer. Then just keep adding the depths of each pier and press the button to keep adding the answers.

NOTE: The calculator will keep adding the inputs to the list amount that is required for all your quantities. The total answer can be saved to the job folder for each construction project. If you need to delete or edit an answer, just click on that line.
